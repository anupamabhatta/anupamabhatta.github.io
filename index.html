About Me

Roots

I was born and raised in Kathmandu, Nepal 🇳🇵, where I learned the value of community and resilience. Fun fact: In Nepal, large-scale data collection is still emerging—we don’t have vast historical datasets, so much of our understanding comes from oral histories and lived experiences. This perspective has shaped how I approach data science: not just as numbers, but as narratives waiting to be uncovered.

Journey

I moved to the U.S. in 2016 for college and earned my degree in Applied Data Analytics and Economics from Wesleyan College. After graduating, I joined Loyal, a healthcare technology company, where I took on increasing responsibilities in enterprise data operations, eventually leading a team and overseeing large-scale API integrations across major healthcare systems.

Today

At UMSI, I focus on machine learning, natural language processing, and network analysis, applying statistical modeling, classification techniques, and graph-based methods to uncover insights in complex datasets. My work includes predictive modeling, text analysis, and interactive visualizations, using tools like scikit-learn, NLTK, PyTorch, NetworkX, and Tableau to build scalable, data-driven solutions.
